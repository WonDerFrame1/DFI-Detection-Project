What is a Deepfake (image)?

Deepfake (image) refers to AI technology used to create or alter human face images, often using deepfake techniques to generate faces of people who don’t exist or modify real people’s images. These synthetic images can be highly realistic, making it difficult for the human eye to distinguish between real and fake content. The advancement of this technology raises ethical questions and presents challenges for authentication in journalism, law enforcement, and social media platforms.

Possible effects

The use of Deepfake (image) can lead to deception, the spread of misinformation, and violations of privacy. It may also reduce trust in technology and result in legal disputes. Misuse of Deepfake images can trigger public panic, damage reputations, and cause psychological harm to the individuals involved. These consequences highlight the urgent need for education, public awareness, and stricter enforcement of ethical guidelines in AI development and deployment. Therefore, it is essential to regulate and use Deepfake technology with caution.
